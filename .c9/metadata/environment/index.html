Is a song's valence linked to it's danceability & energy?
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n\n \n\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
A number of musical features are used by Spotify to describe a song. For the Top 100 songs of 2018, let’s look at their energy, danceability and valence. Spotify measures energy from from 0.0 to 1.0 and represents a perceptual\n measure of intensity and activity. An energetic track will feel loud, fast and noisy. Death metal, for example, has high energy while a Bach prelude will have low energy.
Danceability is measure by how suitable a track is\n for dancing, based on certain musical elements including beat strength, tempo, rhythm stability and regularity. A value of 0.0 is least danceable and 1.0 is most danceable.
A track with high valence will sound more positive\n (e.g. cheerful, happy) and a track with low valance will sound more negative (e.g. sad, angry). It’s also measured 0.0 to 1.0.
\n

\n

Valence is positively correlated with danceability and energy. Happy songs make people energetic and want to dance so the correlation between these factors and valence makes sense.
